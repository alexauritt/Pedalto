Q. What was the first name? What was the name on the ticket you voted? A. Henry Clausen.

Q. Was there anything else on the ticket? A. No, sir.

Q. Just simply Henry Clausen? A. Henry Clausen.

Q. Who gave you the tickets? A. I.got them from my good friend.

Q. Who was that? A. Louis Hoetzle.

Q. Did you read the ticket? A. Yes. I saw on it the name Henry Clausen.

Q. Didn't you look at the other tickets he gave you? A. No, sir.

Q. How many did he give you? A. I think there was six. Q. Was there a rubber band around them when he handed them to you? A. Yes, sir. Q. Who took that band off? A. At the office. Q. The inspector? A. Yes, sir.

Q. That band remained on the tickets till you handed them to the inspector? A. Yes, sir.

Q. Was that little rubber band on them when you got them? A. Yes, sir. I took it off at the office.

Q. He handed you a little bunch of tickets to vote? A. Yes, sir.

. Q. There was a little rubber band around them? A. Yes, sir. Q. Was the band on them when he handed them to you? A. Yes, sir.

Q. You didn't take it off ? A. No, sir.

Q. The man at the ballot box took it off, didn't he? A. No, sir. I didn't see that.

Q. Well, this little rubber band was on when, you handed in the tickets, wasn't it? A. Yes, sir,

Q. Did you examine the tickets? A. No, sir.

Q. You didn't examine any of them? A. No, sir.

Q. You didn't take these tickets out of the little rubber band? A. No, sir, I did not look at them.

Q. You just took these tickets from your friend and handed them to the inspector? A. Yes, sir; I gave them right off.

Q. You are positive that you didn't look at any of them? A. Yes, sir.
